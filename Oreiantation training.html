Oreiantation TRAINING

Orientation training, also known as onboarding or induction training, is the process of introducing new employees to an organization, its culture, policies, procedures, and their specific job roles.
This training helps new hires acclimate to their new workplace and understand the expectations and resources available to them.
Here are some key aspects and benefits of orientation training:

Key Aspects of Orientation Training

Introduction to the Organization:

Company History and Mission:

of the organization’s history, mission, values, and vision.

Organizational Structure:

Explanation of the company's structure, including departments, teams, and key personnel.

Policies and Procedures:

Workplace Policies:

Detailed information on workplace policies, including attendance, dress code, and code of conduct.

Health and Safety:

Training on workplace safety protocols, emergency procedures, and health guidelines.

Role and Responsibilities:

Job Expectations:

Clarification of the new hire’s role, responsibilities, and performance expectations.

Work Processes:

Introduction to the specific processes, tools, and technologies relevant to their job.

Workplace Culture:

Company Culture:

Insights into the organizational culture, including norms, values, and social aspects.

Team Integration:

Opportunities to meet and interact with team members and colleagues.

Administrative Information:

HR Procedures:

Information on payroll, benefits, time tracking, and other administrative details.

IT Setup:

on setting up and using company IT systems, including email, software, and hardware.

Resources and Support:

Training Programs:

Overview of ongoing training and development opportunities.

Support Networks:

to mentors, buddies, or support resources available to new hires.
